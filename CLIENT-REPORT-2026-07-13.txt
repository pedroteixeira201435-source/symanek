SYMANEK SUITE
Client Report — What Your Platform Does
Prepared for: Symanek Specialized College
Date: 13 July 2026
Stage: Working demonstration (clickable prototype). Live data and payments
       come in the next build phase.

============================================================
IN ONE PARAGRAPH
============================================================

Symanek Suite is a single, integrated platform to run your entire institution —
from the day a student applies, through registration, classes, fees, exams and
graduation, plus the staff, finance, library, residence and canteen operations
behind it. It is built specifically for Namibian higher education: fees, tax and
payroll follow NamRA and the Labour Act, and accreditation follows NCHE/NQA/NTA.
Everyone sees only what their role allows. This report explains, in plain terms,
what the platform now does for each group of people who use it.

============================================================
FOR YOUR LEADERSHIP (Principal / Director / Board)
============================================================

- One dashboard for the health of the whole institution: enrolment, fee
  collection, cash position and a prioritised list of what needs attention.
- Full academic lifecycle in one place: Admissions → Registration → Exams →
  Graduation, with clear rules at every step.
- Namibian compliance built in: NamRA tax, NCHE statutory returns, and
  programme accreditation status (NQA/NTA) tracked in the system.
- Configurable for your institution type — the platform can be tailored (for a
  vocational college, a full university, or distance learning), turning modules
  on or off to match how you operate.
- Complete audit trail of sensitive actions (who did what, and when).

============================================================
FOR YOUR STUDENTS (Student Portal)
============================================================

Students log in to a portal of their own and can:
- Register for courses themselves, with the system automatically checking
  eligibility — outstanding fees, prerequisites, class capacity (offering a
  waitlist when full) and the credit limit — and showing the cost as they go.
- See their marks, grade point average and academic transcript.
- Track their progress towards their qualification, requirement by requirement.
- View their timetable, their invoices and sponsorships, and pay online.
- See any holds on their record and request official documents (transcript,
  proof of registration).

New applicants also get their own online application: they submit and then
track the status of their application without needing to visit the office.

============================================================
FOR YOUR LECTURERS
============================================================

- A personal workspace: their own timetable, class lists and gradebook.
- Courseware (online learning): publish materials, set assignments, and receive
  and grade student submissions.
- Continuous-assessment and exam marks feed into a controlled results process —
  once the exam board publishes, marks lock and flow into student transcripts.
- Daily attendance, leave requests and payslips, all self-service.

============================================================
FOR YOUR ADMINISTRATION AND FINANCE
============================================================

- Admissions: a visual application pipeline for the 2027 intake, document
  checklists and capacity per programme.
- Registrar (academic records): course registration, examinations logistics
  (timetable, venues, seating, invigilation) and graduation.
- Graduation & clearance: a student can only receive a certificate once finance,
  library and academic requirements are all cleared — checked automatically.
- Finance: fees, invoices, payments, debtor follow-up, payment plans, budgets
  and sponsorships (NSFAF/NTA/corporate), with claims tracked to the funder.
- Accounting: double-entry ledger with a Namibian tax engine (company tax, VAT
  on mixed supplies — tuition exempt, canteen taxable — and provisional tax).
- HR & Payroll: staff records, contracts, recruitment, and payroll calculated to
  the current PAYE tables, Social Security and the VET levy.

============================================================
FOR YOUR CAMPUS OPERATIONS
============================================================

- Accommodation / residences: room occupancy, allocation and waitlist.
- Library: catalogue, lending, reservations and fines.
- Canteen: a point-of-sale till (prepaid student accounts), stock control and
  end-of-shift reconciliation, with sales reporting for management.

============================================================
HOW ACCESS AND SAFETY WORK
============================================================

- Each person signs in to a workspace limited to their role — a student never
  sees institutional finance; a lecturer sees only their own classes; the
  canteen till is a locked, full-screen point of sale with no admin access.
- Every important action is recorded for accountability.

============================================================
WHAT THIS IS TODAY — AND WHAT COMES NEXT
============================================================

Today you have a complete, clickable demonstration: you can walk through every
screen and every workflow exactly as staff and students would. This lets you
confirm the design and the workflows before we invest in the engine behind it.

Because this stage is a demonstration, the information shown is sample data and
actions are not yet saved permanently. The next phase adds the "engine":
- secure sign-in with real accounts,
- permanent storage of every record,
- online payments through a Namibian payment provider,
- document uploads (certificates, transcripts, course material),
- and email/SMS notifications.

The foundations for that engine have already been prepared, so the next phase is
about connecting a live system to screens you have already approved — not
rebuilding.

We would value your feedback on the workflows in this demonstration; it directly
shapes the build that follows.
